About Me
I am a 2nd year PhD candidate in the Department of Biological Chemistry at the Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine. My dissertation research in the lab of Dr. Mollie K Meffert examines how miRNAs mediate post-transcriptional gene regulation in the mammalian central nervous system. I use a method called cross-linking and immunoprecipitation (CLIP) to study the interactions
between miRNAs and target RNAs within the RNA-induced
silencing complex. A technique developed by
several labs now makes it possible to directly
ligate miRNAs with their targets, which allows
for the unambiguous mapping of miRNA-target
interactions. I hope to use this technique to map
the miRNA-target network in the brain of a
mouse model of autism and also within various
populations of neurons in the mouse brain.
